Coriolix

Coriolix is the onboard data logging and visualization tool used on OSU research vessels. It allows for real-time and historical data analysis from various shipboard sensors.

  3. Click "Add to Graph" to visualize

Underway Data & Network Systems

This section provides information about the ship's data infrastructure for science operations.

UDP Data Feeds

Various sensors broadcast data over the internal network using UDP. Refer to the table below for port numbers and protocols. Use the port numbers to listen unless otherwise specified.

Feed Name Port Number Protocol Notes
GPS Position 5001 UDP Main navigation data
Sound Velocity 5002 UDP Used by sonar systems

KVM & Video Matrix System

The ship uses a matrix switcher to route video and keyboard signals to multiple workstations. Operators can switch sources from lab stations or the bridge. Contact Marine Techs for assistance.

Time Server Access

The ship provides an onboard NTP server to synchronize science and nav systems. Details on IP and access credentials will be provided soon.

Accessing Shared Drives

Shared drives for science data can be accessed over SMB or NFS. Paths, user credentials, and mount instructions will be posted at cruise start.

See the Mounting Drives Guide for detailed instructions.

Wire Tension Monitoring

Winch systems provide live tension readings via serial outputs. These feeds are viewable from the lab and bridge. Contact a Marine Tech for setup instructions.

Disembarking & At-Sea Tips and Procedures

Welcome aboard! Please review the following important information to ensure your safety and smooth operations during the cruise.

Muster Location & Safety Briefing

All personnel must attend the safety briefing and muster drill within the first hour of boarding. The muster station is located on the starboard side, main deck (Deck 3), near the aft stairs. Pay close attention to emergency signals and procedures explained by the safety officer.

Before Cruise Procedures

  • Medical Clearance: Submit all required medical forms and clearances to the cruise coordinator before embarkation.
  • Gear Check: Ensure all personal protective equipment (PPE) and necessary scientific gear are properly packed and labeled.
  • Briefings: Attend all pre-cruise science and operations briefings to familiarize yourself with ship protocols and scientific objectives.
  • Communications Setup: Check in with the IT department to set up network access and any required software tools.
  • Emergency Contacts: Familiarize yourself with the onboard emergency contact list and procedures.

At Sea Etiquette & Tips

  • Respect quiet hours in crew quarters and labs.
  • Keep walkways clear and secure your equipment during rough seas.
  • Follow all safety signage and crew instructions promptly.
  • Report any safety hazards or medical concerns immediately to the bridge or medical officer.

For further details, consult the ship’s safety manual or contact the Marine Techs team.
